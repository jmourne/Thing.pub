Thing.pub

Secure Manuscript Distribution | Line-Level Feedback | Reader Intelligence

The Mission

This platform is a proprietary environment built for authors and their manuscripts. It bridges the gap between extreme IP protection and deep reader analytics, allowing authors to observe how readers engage with their work while gathering granular, line-level critiques.

Forensic Security Protocol

To ensure 100% risk-mitigation, work on these security features takes top priority.

Device Locking

Hardware fingerprinting restricts access to two unique devices per access code. No account sharing. Reduces chances of piracy.

Dynamic Watermarking

Non-removable watermarking tied to each unique reader ID. Should a leak happen, you'll know exactly who is responsible.

Anti-Piracy Hardening

Disabled text selection, right-clicking, printing, and downloading.

Core Features

Reader Feedback

Line-Level Commenting

Allows readers to highlight specific sections of lines for direct feedback using a proprietary commenting engine. This engine prevents text-selection and copying, while still attributing feedback to specific text sections.

Threaded Dialogue

Authors can choose to engage in direct conversations via threaded comments with readers within the text.

ARC Review Prompts

Automated requests triggered upon completion to capture sentiment while the readers are still fresh with the story in their mind.

'End of' Surveys

Short-form prompts at key narrative beats to track real-time emotional resonance can be set by authors per scene/chapter/act/etc.

Author Dashboard

Engagement Heatmaps

Visualize narrative drop-off points with dwell-time tracking.

Compiled Sentiment

Centralized oversight of reader notes categorized by intensity, context, scenes and chapters.

Completion Metrics

Monitor manuscript momentum and reader retention through the final act.

Private Reader Access

Manage individual permissions, audit logs, and keep internal notes for every invited reader.

Reader Protocol

Transparency and mutual trust are foundational to the Thing.pub security model. All invited contributors must adhere to the proprietary distribution standards, which govern device usage, IP protection, and feedback etiquette.
